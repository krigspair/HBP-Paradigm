# HBP Part XXIV: Culture-in-Being (Культура-в-бытии)
## Рабочий файл | v0.0 (pre-research) | 02.03.2026

---

## 1. Обоснование модуля

Культура в HBP до сих пор представлена точечно:
- P-Aggression (Tier 3, §3.2.2): культурный контекст агрессии (honor cultures, face-saving)
- L-HBP (J7): код-свитчинг L1/L2 как precision manipulation
- L-HBP (J6): Redemption/Contamination sequences (McAdams)
- TTOM упоминается в столпах, но не развёрнут

Системного модуля нет. Культура — зонтичная мета-тема, требующая отдельного Part в System Map.

---

## 2. Ключевой тезис (привязка к ядру)

**Культура = shared generative model**: коллективные priors, передаваемые через язык, ритуал, нарратив, институты. В терминах FEP — макро-уровень Software (Input → Software интернализация).

Культура формирует:
- **Prior preferences** по всем 6 FM (что можно хотеть, чувствовать, кем быть, ради чего жить, как быть в цифре, как относиться к смерти)
- **Precision policy** — что считается значимым сигналом, а что — шум
- **Attractor landscape** — какие паттерны бытия доступны, какие запрещены

## 3. Карта подтем и привязка к ядру

| Подтема | HBP-привязка | Ключевые авторы |
|---|---|---|
| Высоко/низкоконтекстные культуры (Hall) | Precision allocation в коммуникации: high-context = implicit priors, low-context = explicit PE | E.T. Hall, Nisbett |
| Религия / духовность | FM4 (смысл) + FM6 (бытие-в-конечности); религия как аттрактор — стабилизирующий или ригидный | Pargament, Frankl, Лэнгле |

| Мифология / нарратив | Мифы как коллективные generative models; Redemption/Contamination (уже в L-HBP J6) | McAdams, Campbell, Jung |
| Искусство | Aesthetic experience = оптимальный prediction error; art as epistemic foraging | Vessel, Starr, Menninghaus |
| Традиции / ритуал | Ритуал = precision-boosting через повторение + social synchrony → снижение collective uncertainty | Hobson, Legare |
| Коллективизм / индивидуализм | FM3 (Self) calibration: коллективизм = relational self priors > individual self priors | Markus & Kitayama, Hofstede |

---

## 4. Что модуль меняет в парадигме

1. **Этиология расширяется**: Input failure получает культурный слой. Культура может быть патогенным Input (токсичные collective priors: стигма, honor killing norms) или протективным (ритуал как негэнтропийный механизм)
2. **Каждое измерение бытия культурно модулировано**: FM1 «могу ли я быть здесь» — решается культурой принадлежности/изгнания. FM6 — культура определяет отношение к смерти
3. **Терапевтические форматы**: E-HBP и Ed-HBP получают культурную калибровку. P-HBP — невозможен без культурного слоя
4. **L-HBP**: код-свитчинг L1/L2 — прямой мост к билингвальной/мультикультурной идентичности

## 5. Архитектура модуля (целевая)

- **Tier 1**: Культура как shared generative model (FEP-формализация, TTOM)
- **Tier 2**: Карта культурных модуляций по 6 FM + этиологическая роль (Input/Software)
- **Tier 3**: Клинические импликации — culture-bound syndromes, кросс-культурная терапия, религиозный копинг, искусство как терапия
- **APV-карты**: H-Culture-1…N

## 6. План Deep Research (5 запусков)

### Почему подтемами, а не единым блоком:
1. **Лимит контекста DR** (~30–60 стр.): единый запрос → энциклопедия без глубины (нарушение правила 4)
2. **Качество FEP-формализации**: каждая подтема требует отдельного формального мостика
3. **Проверенный паттерн**: мотивация, агрессия, эмоции, воля — всё делалось подтемами
4. **Инкрементальный патчинг**: подтема → Tier 1–3 → APV → consistency check → патч

### Последовательность:

#### DR-Culture-1: Культура как shared generative model

```
You are a research assistant for the HBP (Human Being Psychology) paradigm. 

Task: Comprehensive literature review on CULTURE AS A SHARED GENERATIVE MODEL through the lens of Free Energy Principle (FEP) and Active Inference.

Focus areas:
1. Veissière et al. (2020) "Thinking Through Other Minds" (TTOM) — full formalization of cultural priors as shared expectations. How cultural norms function as hierarchical priors constraining individual prediction error minimization.
2. Henrich's "cultural brain hypothesis" and cultural evolution as collective Bayesian updating.
3. Hesp et al. "deeply felt affect" and cultural shaping of affective priors.
4. Ramstead, Veissière, Kirmayer — cultural affordances as shared precision policies.
5. Bruineberg & Rietveld — skilled intentionality and socio-cultural landscape of affordances.
6. Cultural learning as empirical prior acquisition: imitation, pedagogy, epistemic trust (Fonagy, Csibra & Gergely natural pedagogy).
7. Enculturation vs. socialization — FEP-compatible distinction.

Output format:
- For each source: core claim, formal mechanism (in FEP terms: priors, precision, prediction error, active inference, model evidence), empirical support, limitations.
- Synthesis table: author → FEP mechanism → cultural phenomenon explained.
- Critical gaps: what is NOT yet formalized.
- Minimum 30 sources, prioritize 2018–2026.
```

#### DR-Culture-2: Измерения культуры → precision и Self

```
You are a research assistant for the HBP (Human Being Psychology) paradigm.

Task: Review cultural dimensions frameworks and map them onto FEP/Active Inference mechanisms.

Focus areas:
1. Hall's high-context vs. low-context cultures — reframe as implicit vs. explicit precision allocation in communication. What does this mean for prediction error processing in therapy?
2. Hofstede's dimensions (individualism/collectivism, power distance, uncertainty avoidance, masculinity/femininity, long-term orientation, indulgence) — map each to FEP constructs (prior preferences, precision weighting, exploration-exploitation trade-off).
3. Markus & Kitayama independent vs. interdependent self-construal — implications for FM3 ("May I be myself?") and self-model architecture in active inference.
4. Schwartz's cultural value orientations.
5. Nisbett's "Geography of Thought" — analytic vs. holistic cognition as different generative model architectures.
6. WEIRD problem in psychology (Henrich, Heine, Norenzayan) — implications for universality claims in psychotherapy.
7. Cross-cultural differences in emotion recognition, expression rules (Matsumoto), alexithymia prevalence.

Output format:
- Mapping table: Cultural dimension → FEP mechanism → Clinical implication for psychotherapy.
- Evidence for cultural modulation of each of the 6 existential dimensions (FM1–FM6): Being-in-the-world, Life, Selfhood, Meaning, Being-in-digital, Being-in-finitude.
- Minimum 30 sources, prioritize empirical studies and meta-analyses.
```

6. Religious trauma / spiritual abuse — as pathogenic Input failure with specific Software consequences.
7. Meditation and contemplative practices — active inference accounts (Lutz, Laukkonen, Deane).
8. Indigenous and non-Western healing systems — shamanism, ayahuasca, traditional healing as cultural technologies for phase transitions.

Output format:
- For each topic: core mechanism, FEP formalization, empirical evidence, clinical relevance.
- Table: Religious/spiritual phenomenon → FEP mechanism → FM dimension → Therapeutic implication.
- Minimum 30 sources, balance Western and cross-cultural perspectives.
```

#### DR-Culture-4: Этнопсихология и culture-bound syndromes

```
You are a research assistant for the HBP (Human Being Psychology) paradigm.

Task: Review ethnopsychology and culture-bound syndromes through FEP and dynamic systems theory.

Focus areas:
1. Culture-bound syndromes (DSM-5 "cultural concepts of distress": ataque de nervios, taijin kyofusho, susto, hikikomori, koro, dhat, amok) — reframe each as LOCAL ATTRACTOR in a culturally shaped attractor landscape. What cultural priors create the basin of attraction for each syndrome?
2. Kirmayer's "cultural biology of the brain" — how culture shapes neural prediction machinery.
3. Vygotsky's cultural-historical psychology — zone of proximal development as scaffolded precision, internalization as prior acquisition. Connection to HBP's Software etiology.
4. Cole, Ratner, Shweder — cultural psychology vs. cross-cultural psychology distinction.
5. Kleinman's explanatory models — patient's generative model of illness vs. clinician's generative model. Therapeutic alliance as model alignment.
6. Cultural adaptation of psychotherapy — what works, what doesn't (Benish, Quintana, Wampold meta-analysis; Griner & Smith). FEP framing: therapy must engage the client's cultural priors, not override them.
7. Migration, acculturation stress, cultural bereavement — as massive prediction error from generative model mismatch (Berry's acculturation model reframed).
8. Collective trauma and intergenerational transmission — epigenetic and narrative mechanisms.

Output format:
- Table: Culture-bound syndrome → Cultural priors creating it → FEP mechanism → HBP etiology category.
- Synthesis of cultural adaptation principles for D-HBP, C-HBP, B-HBP formats.
- Minimum 30 sources, include non-Western authors where possible.
```

#### DR-Culture-5: Искусство, мифология, ритуал

```
You are a research assistant for the HBP (Human Being Psychology) paradigm.

Task: Review art, mythology, and ritual as psychological phenomena through predictive processing, FEP, and existential dimensions.

Focus areas:
1. Neuroaesthetics and predictive processing: art as optimal prediction error (Vessel, Menninghaus "Distancing-Embracing" model; Van de Cruys & Wagemans predictive coding account of aesthetic experience; Sarasso et al. perturbational complexity).
2. Art therapy — mechanisms through FEP lens: externalization of generative model, safe prediction error exploration, metaphor as precision manipulation.
3. Mythology as collective generative model: Campbell's monomyth, Jung's archetypes reframed as deep cultural priors (hyper-priors). McAdams' narrative identity — redemption/contamination sequences (already in L-HBP).
4. Ritual as precision-boosting mechanism: Hobson et al. "The Psychology of Rituals", Legare & Souza — causal opacity and precision. Ritual synchrony and social bonding (Tarr, Launay, Dunbar).
5. Music and predictive processing: Koelsch, Pearce & Wiggins — music as pure prediction error dynamics. Musical chills as precision surges.
6. Literature and narrative as simulation of alternative generative models (Mar & Oatley "fiction as social simulation"; Oatley "Such Stuff as Dreams"). Bibliotherapy mechanisms.
7. Play (Huizinga, Winnicott, Panksepp PLAY system) — play as safe active inference with reduced precision on outcomes. Connection to transitional space (already in HBP K7).
8. Digital art, VR, AI-generated art — FM5 (Being-in-digital) implications.

Output format:
- For each domain: core mechanism, FEP formalization, empirical evidence, therapeutic application in HBP formats.
- Mapping table: Cultural practice → FEP mechanism → FM dimension → HBP format applicability.
- Minimum 30 sources, prioritize computational/neuroaesthetic empirical work.
